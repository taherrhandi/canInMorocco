Do You Need a Visa for Morocco?

Whether or not you need a visa for Morocco depends on your nationality. Citizens of several countries are exempt from Morocco visa requirements – which means they can travel to Morocco without having to obtain a visa beforehand. This includes the US, EU, Japan, Australia, etc. See the visa policy of Morocco below.

Countries whose citizens do not need visa to enter into Morocco:

Morocco is a quite open country: the citizens of 70 countries are exempted from entry visa into Morocco.

Getting Round Rabat

RABAT, A MODERN CAPITAL WITH DEEP-ROOTED HISTORy

Rabat is a cultural city deep-rooted in history. Many masterpieces decorate its streets and squares. Visit the Kasbah des Oudayas which is a majestic and splendid architecture embellished by the surrounding gardens. Not far from the walls, stand the imposing walls of the Chellah ; a necropolis from the time of the Merinids. Crossing the walls is like entering another world ; walk among ancient vestiges, gardens and storks.

Rabat is also a modern eco-responsible capital with its green spaces. Beautiful parks await you, such as the botanical test garden or the Exotic Gardens of Bouknadel which is a few kilometres from the city. Rabat boasts an outstanding coastline ; on the Atlantic Ocean shores, it has kilometres of well equipped beaches that lead to the neighbouring city of Casablanca.

With its modern infrastructures and various festivals, Rabat is a living city. Airport, tram, shopping centres, cafés and restaurants, all the amenities are within your reach. Revel in bustling vibes of music that Rabat celebrates like no other city; from Mawazine, to Jazz au Chellah and many others that fill the atmosphere with sounds and rhythms from all over the world !

Rabat is a city where the treasures of the past blend with the most modern and environmentally friendly achievements.

discover morrocan foods

Moroccan cuisine is a flavorful fusion of Mediterranean, Arabic, and Berber influences. Known for its aromatic spices, tagines, and couscous, Moroccan food tantalizes the taste buds with a perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors. From savory dishes like lamb tagine with apricots to sweet treats like honey-soaked pastries, Moroccan cuisine offers a culinary journey that is rich in tradition and diverse in its offerings.

rabat shopping

Rabat offers a diverse shopping experience, blending traditional souks with modern malls. In the vibrant souks of the medina, shoppers can immerse themselves in a maze of narrow alleys filled with stalls selling traditional crafts, textiles, spices, and more. Bargaining is a common practice here. For a more contemporary shopping experience, Rabat's modern malls provide a range of international and local brands, along with restaurants and entertainment options. Whether you seek the charm of traditional markets or the convenience of modern shopping, Rabat caters to every shopper's preference.
